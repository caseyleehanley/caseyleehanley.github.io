About Me

Hi, I’m Casey. I'm a software developer living in Wisconsin.

I've been programming since 2012, back when I was deciding between Computer Science and Industrial Design in college. I chose Computer Science and never looked back, but I like to think I've retained a half-decent design eye from my brief exploration in an alternative field.

I began my career out of college at Apple, where I worked on a variety of web-based tools. Since 2017, I've worked at FSB Companies in Chicago, where I work with small businesses to help them grow their operations using software. Most recently, I've been focused on building apps for iOS and related platforms using Swift, including both client- and server-side components.

I'm enthusiastic about the future of the Swift programming language, especially the more widespread adoption of Swift on the server. I'm also very passionate about the Mac, and have been since I saved up enough money to buy my first MacBook Pro in 2008. My favorite programming languages are Swift and Go, but I've also enjoyed the limited time I've spent learning Objective-C.

I'm often happiest when I'm building things, and most of the time that means writing code in Xcode. When I'm not building things, you'll probably find me walking or running around outside, exploring the streets and parks of Madison, Wisconsin.
