Slow and Steady

December 18, 2022

As it goes with any creative endeavor, the vision at the start can sometimes fail to resemble the finished product. And that's often a good thing! The best products are relentlessly refined over and over again with numerous missteps along the way. I truly believe that's how great things become so great. While I originally imagined a more frequent posting schedule for this blog, it's pretty clear that bar was too high for me (my last post was over 6 months ago). C'est la vie. I venture on anyway!

Over the past 6 months, I've gone back and forth on the title for this blog. While the current one is similar, the original title was "Objective-C(asey)" with a subtitle of "Adventures in Mac Programming." While I still like the throwback to Objective-C and the old way of programming for Apple platforms, I'm hardly an authority on the topic. In fact, I'm far from it. Who am I kidding? I've never written a Mac app. I barely know Objective-C (although I've enjoyed learning for fun). Something just didn't feel right. It wasn't authentic. As much as I want to be an expert on the topic, I'm not quite there yet, and I'm not in the business of fooling people. I do have a unique perspective in some ways, though. I really like the Mac. And I admire Apple in general. I've also been programming professionally for six or so years. There has to be something there, right? So, how can I broaden my scope of potential topics, while also keeping a somewhat specific niche?

Well... I dropped the "Mac" in the subtitle. As silly as it is, I feel like it really takes the pressure off of thinking about topic ideas. Imagining a slightly more general audience allows me to think more freely about what I want this to become. Still nerdy programming stuff (and let's be honest, still probably about Apple), just less specific. I also took my name out of the title. I don't have a good explanation for doing this— it just felt right. The new title, "@objcturtle", now serves multiple purposes. It's obscure enough to be available on all the social things (and as a domain), so I can have the same handle everywhere without adding random numbers at the end. It still contains the reference to Objective-C that I was originally going for, albeit now with a bit more subtlety. And lastly, it includes the name of an animal that perfectly encapsulates the rate at which I've been making progress on this project!

Here's to new ideas and more reasonable expectations, all with the same lofty nerd goals. Maybe I'll even write a post related to programming next time! What a thought.
